\subsection{The Ising Model}\label{sec:ising-model}

In the previous section, we established a bridge between QUBO optimization problems and quantum systems by constructing a Hamiltonian whose ground state corresponds to the optimal solution of the QUBO problem.

\highspace
However, the Hamiltonian introduced so far is mainly a conceptual construction. Real quantum optimization devices are typically described using a different, but closely related, mathematical model known as the \textbf{Ising model}.

\highspace
The Ising model originates from statistical mechanics and was originally developed to describe magnetic materials. Today, it plays a central role in quantum optimization because many quantum annealers and optimization-oriented quantum devices naturally implement \textbf{Ising Hamiltonians}.

\highspace
Therefore, before studying quantum annealing, we need to understand how the concepts introduced so far relate to the Ising formulation and how QUBO problems can be represented within that framework.
